AU is a live video installation that allows viewers to experience the concept of a shared present between two observers separated by one Astronomical Unit (AU)    +

Aristarchus of Samo -some 2200 years ago- roughly calculated the distance from the earth, to the moon, and the sun. His work unlocked a series of subsequent measurements that culminated in what today is referred to as the astronomical unit (au).
The astronomical unit measures the approximate distance between the Earth and the Sun, which averages to roughly 150 million kilometers, depending on the time of the year it is measured. This large number is easier to digest as 499 light/seconds, or the time it takes light to travel one astronomical unit.
Because of this, we now know that when two observers are separated by large distances, it’s impossible for them to observe the same event in real time. In other words, they can’t share the same present.
